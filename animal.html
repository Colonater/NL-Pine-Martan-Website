Introduction

The Newfoundland Pine Martan (Martes Americana Atarta) is a small mammel only found on the island of Newfoundland, Canada. It is a subspecies of the American Pine Martan and is know for its beautiful and elusive nature.

Physical-characteristics

The Newfoundland Pine Martan has a Reddish-Brown fur with a cream colored throat and Chest. It has a long, bushy tail and sharp claws for climbing trees. Adult Males can weigh up to 2.5 pounds and measure up to 18 inchs in length

Habitat

The Newfoundland Pine Martan is found in mature coniferous and mixed woods, particularly in areas with good tree cover and thick underbrush. They are also known to use old bird nests and other natural cavitities for den sites.

Biology

Pine martens have a diet that mainly consists of small mammals, birds, insects, and fruit. They are solitary animals and have a home range of around 4-5 square kilometers. They are also excellent climbers and swimmers, which allows them to move easily through their forest habitats.

Behavior

The Newfoundland Pine Marten is active during the day and night, but is most active at dawn and dusk. They are solitary animals, execpt during the breeding season. They are skilled climbers and swimmers, and primarily eat small mammals, birds and berries.

Conservation

The Newfoundland Pine Martian is considered a special concern by the Canadian Goverment. Habitat loss and trapping for its valuble fur are the main threats to the species. Conservation efforts include habitat protection and management, and regulations on trapping.
